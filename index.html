About

The Bhutanese Physical Society is dedicated to promoting physics education, research, and collaboration in Bhutan. The Society engages students, schools, educators, and researchers through outreach programs, workshops, seminars, and conferences to foster curiosity, scientific literacy, and a strong academic community.

By building national and international collaborations, the Society supports the development of physics-driven solutions aligned with Bhutan‚Äôs cultural values, Gross National Happiness, and sustainable development goals.

Vision

The Bhutanese Physical Society envisions a Bhutan where the understanding of nature through physics serves the well-being of people, the protection of the environment, and the prosperity of future generations.

Rooted in Bhutan‚Äôs cultural values and the principles of Gross National Happiness, the Society seeks to advance physics education, research, and innovation in ways that are ethical, mindful, and aligned with national priorities.

Through the responsible application of physics‚Äîparticularly in renewable energy, climate resilience, and sustainable technologies‚Äîthe Society aspires to support Bhutan‚Äôs transition toward a knowledge-based, environmentally harmonious, and self-reliant future.

Mission

The mission of the Bhutanese Physical Society is to promote the study, teaching, and application of physics in Bhutan for the benefit of society and the natural environment.

The Society works to strengthen physics education, support research and innovation, and encourage the responsible use of physics-based technologies‚Äîparticularly in renewable energy, climate resilience, and sustainable systems.

Through collaboration with educational institutions, government agencies, industry, and communities, the Society seeks to build national scientific capacity and nurture young physicists in alignment with Bhutan‚Äôs cultural values and Gross National Happiness.

Objectives

  1. Promote physics education through quality teaching and learning resources.
  2. Encourage research in renewable energy, environmental physics, and climate resilience.
  3. Nurture young physicists and researchers through mentorship and training.
  4. Facilitate collaboration among physicists, educators, and institutions.
  5. Provide evidence-based scientific input for national development.
  6. Promote ethical and responsible applications of physics.
  7. Organize lectures, workshops, conferences, and outreach programs.
