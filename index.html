Bhutanese Physical Society

Advancing Physics for a Sustainable and Knowledge-Based Bhutan

Vision

The Bhutanese Physical Society envisions a Bhutan where the understanding of nature through physics serves the well-being of people, the protection of the environment, and the prosperity of future generations.

Rooted in Bhutan’s cultural values and the principles of Gross National Happiness, the Society seeks to advance physics education, research, and innovation in ways that are ethical, mindful, and aligned with national priorities.

Through the responsible application of physics—particularly in renewable energy, climate resilience, and sustainable technologies—the Society aspires to support Bhutan’s transition toward a knowledge-based, environmentally harmonious, and self-reliant future, while fostering collaboration between scientists, educators, policymakers, and communities.

Mission

The mission of the Bhutanese Physical Society is to promote the study, teaching, and application of physics in Bhutan for the benefit of society and the natural environment.

The Society works to strengthen physics education, support research and innovation, and encourage the responsible use of physics-based technologies—particularly in renewable energy, climate resilience, and sustainable systems.

Through collaboration with educational institutions, government agencies, industry, and communities, the Society seeks to build national scientific capacity, nurture young physicists, and contribute evidence-based knowledge to national development in a manner consistent with Bhutan’s cultural values and the principles of Gross National Happiness.

Objectives

  1. To promote physics education in Bhutan by supporting quality teaching, curriculum development, and learning resources at schools, colleges, and universities.
  2. To encourage and support physics research, with particular emphasis on renewable energy, environmental physics, climate resilience, and technologies relevant to Bhutan’s sustainable development.
  3. To build national scientific capacity by nurturing young physicists, researchers, and educators through mentorship, training programs, and academic exchange.
  4. To facilitate collaboration and knowledge exchange among physicists, engineers, educators, policymakers, and institutions within Bhutan and internationally.
  5. To provide evidence-based scientific input on physics-related matters of national importance, when requested, in support of informed decision-making and responsible development.
  6. To promote the ethical and responsible application of physics in ways that respect Bhutan’s cultural values, environmental integrity, and the principles of Gross National Happiness.
  7. To organize scientific activities such as lectures, workshops, conferences, and outreach programs to advance public understanding of physics and its role in society.

Core Values & Code of Ethics

A. Core Values

  1. Service to Society: The Society upholds physics as a means to serve the well-being of the Bhutanese people and the long-term interests of the nation, rather than personal, commercial, or ideological gain.
  2. Environmental Responsibility: The Society is committed to the protection of Bhutan’s natural environment and promotes scientific practices and technologies that respect ecological balance and intergenerational responsibility.
  3. Scientific Integrity: The Society values honesty, rigor, transparency, and critical inquiry in all scientific activities, recognizing these as essential foundations of credible and trustworthy physics.
  4. Mindfulness and Ethical Conduct: Guided by mindfulness and responsibility, the Society encourages thoughtful application of scientific knowledge, mindful of social, cultural, and environmental consequences.
  5. Respect for Culture and Tradition: The Society respects Bhutan’s cultural heritage and values and seeks to harmonize scientific advancement with national identity and the principles of Gross National Happiness.
  6. Inclusiveness and Capacity Building: The Society promotes inclusive participation, encourages young scientists, and supports the growth of local expertise across generations and regions.
  7. Collaboration and Mutual Respect: The Society fosters cooperation among individuals and institutions, valuing respectful dialogue, shared learning, and collective progress.

B. Code of Ethics

  1. Professional Conduct:
    • Conduct themselves with honesty, humility, and professionalism
    • Respect diverse viewpoints and maintain collegial relationships
    • Avoid misuse of scientific authority or expertise
  2. Scientific Responsibility:
    • Uphold accuracy and objectivity in research, teaching, and communication
    • Acknowledge limitations and uncertainties in scientific work
    • Avoid fabrication, falsification, or misrepresentation of data
  3. Environmental and Social Ethics:
    • Promote scientific practices that minimize environmental harm
    • Avoid endorsing activities that lead to ecological degradation or unsustainable resource use
    • Consider social and cultural impacts when applying physics-based technologies
  4. Independence and Conflict of Interest:
    • Disclose any financial, institutional, or personal interests that may influence professional judgment
    • Avoid conflicts of interest that compromise scientific integrity or public trust
    • Ensure that Society activities remain independent and non-partisan
  5. Education and Public Engagement:
    • Communicate science responsibly and accurately to students, policymakers, and the public
    • Encourage critical thinking and evidence-based understanding
    • Avoid exaggeration or fear-based messaging
  6. Advisory Role and Public Trust:
    • Offer evidence-based advice without advocacy beyond their expertise
    • Clearly distinguish scientific findings from personal opinions
    • Respect institutional processes and national decision-making structures
  7. Accountability and Review:
    • The Society shall periodically review its ethical standards
    • Members are accountable to the Society’s constitution and ethical guidelines
    • Alleged ethical violations shall be addressed through fair and transparent procedures
