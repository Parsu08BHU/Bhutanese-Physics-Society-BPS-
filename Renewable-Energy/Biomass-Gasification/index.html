Biomass Gasification

Innovative High-Flow Gasifier for Clean Energy

The Challenge

The world currently faces a dual crisis: shrinking fossil fuel reserves and a rapidly depleting carbon budget. Despite the availability of renewable resources, billions of tons of agricultural waste are destroyed through field burning annually. This practice contributes to 40% of regional air pollution and represents a lost energy value of roughly $50 billion.

The Solution: A New Paradigm in Gasification

While traditional gasification has existed since the 1800s, conventional industrial gasifiers are often hindered by their large size, high cost, and issues like tar formation or feedstock blockages.

Sharma’s research introduces a new construction principle for gasifiers. By inverting the traditional causality chain—viewing the gasification process not as a result of oxygen lack, but as the origin of lower oxygen consumption—designers can implement abundant air inlets to create a larger, more homogeneous reaction zone.

Technical Excellence & Performance

  • Compact Prototype: 150 cm tall unit made of stainless steel.
  • High Power Density: 10 MW/m³; a 5-liter reactor produces 50 kW thermal output.
  • Versatile Feedstock: Handles pellets and non-pelletized green waste.
  • Sustainability: Up to 85% CO₂ reduction versus fossil fuels.

Future Outlook: 24/7 Clean Energy

The research proposes a Future Energy Park integrating solar concentration and gasification. Solar energy is used during the day, while the gasifier ensures night-time energy generation.

Applications include off-grid energy, district heating, and biochar production for carbon sequestration and soil enrichment.
